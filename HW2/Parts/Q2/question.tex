The group $\SE{2}$ of planar rigid motions is our first example of a \textbf{semi-direct product}. This structure---where one subgroup ``twists'' another---is fundamental to robotics.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The Translation Group $\mathbb{T}(2)$:} The set of 2D translations forms a group $\mathbb{T}(2)$ under composition. As a matrix Lie group:
\[
\mathbb{T}(2) = \left\{ \begin{bmatrix} I & \bm{t} \\ \bm{0}^T & 1 \end{bmatrix} : \bm{t} \in \R[2] \right\} \cong (\R[2], +)
\]
This group is abelian (translations commute) and isomorphic to $\R[2]$ with vector addition. Its Lie algebra is $\mathfrak{t}(2) \cong \R[2]$.
